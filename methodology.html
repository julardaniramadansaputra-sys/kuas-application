Research Methodology

Simulation Setup, Data Generation, and Model Training Pipeline.

Experimental Setup

Due to the destructive nature of failure testing on real high-power inverters, this research utilizes a Hardware-in-the-Loop (HIL) Simulation approach as a preliminary validation step.

Phase 1: Data Generation (PLECS)

A 3-Phase Inverter model is built in PLECS simulation software. To simulate aging, we inject degradation parameters:

  • Bond-wire lift-off: Simulated by increasing resistance ($R_{on}$) by 5-20%.
  • Solder fatigue: Simulated by increasing thermal impedance ($Z_{th}$).

Phase 2: Pre-processing

Raw data from the simulation (Voltage, Current, Temperature) is sampled at 10 kHz. The data is then normalized using Min-Max Scaling to ensure stability during neural network training.

Phase 3: Model Training

The dataset is split into Training (70%), Validation (15%), and Testing (15%). The LSTM model is trained using the Adam Optimizer with a learning rate of 0.001 to minimize the Mean Squared Error (MSE) loss function.
